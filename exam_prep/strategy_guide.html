Time Management Strategy

165 minutes / 110 questions = ~1.5 minutes per question. Plan for 135 minutes of active work (80 + 40 + 15 across three passes) with a 30-minute review buffer. Many questions will take under a minute if you know the material. Budget that saved time for the hard ones.

Domain Weighting Strategy

Not all domains are equal. Allocate your study time proportionally—but with a bias toward your weakest areas.

D2 — Security Principles
40%
D3 — AI-Assisted Security
24%
D4 — Governance & Compliance
19%
D1 — AI Concepts
17%

Domain 2 is 40% of the exam. This is where you win or lose. Prioritize studying attacks, defenses, and data protection. If you nail Domain 2, you can afford to be weaker elsewhere.

  • Domain 3 (24%) — AI-assisted security is the second biggest area. Know how AI augments SOC workflows, threat detection, and incident response.
  • Domain 4 (19%) — Governance and compliance. Lots of framework memorization: NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act risk levels, responsible AI principles. Use flashcards.
  • Domain 1 (17%) — Foundational AI/ML concepts. Should be the easiest if you understand the fundamentals of how models work.

Question Elimination Techniques

  1. Read the ENTIRE question before looking at answers. Many questions have a twist in the last sentence.
  2. Identify the key verb — identify, prevent, mitigate, implement, describe each imply a different type of answer.
  3. Eliminate obviously wrong answers first. Usually 1–2 are clearly wrong, giving you a 50/50 shot at worst.
  4. For "NOT" questions: find the 3 that DO belong. The odd one out is the answer.
  5. For scenario questions: identify the domain first, then narrow to concepts within that domain.

Watch for absolute words — "always", "never", "only", "all". These are often incorrect because security rarely deals in absolutes.

Watch for "best" or "most" — Multiple answers may be partially correct. Pick the MOST correct or MOST complete one. If stuck between two, re-read the scenario for a keyword that distinguishes them.

Common Traps & Confusing Pairs

CompTIA loves testing concepts that sound similar but have distinct meanings. Memorize these distinctions.

Concept A Concept B Key Distinction
Data Poisoning vs Model Poisoning Poisoning training DATA vs poisoning model WEIGHTS directly
Transparency vs Explainability Can you SEE the process vs can you UNDERSTAND a specific decision
Human-in-the-loop vs Human Oversight Approve EACH decision vs ONGOING monitoring
Encryption In Transit vs At Rest vs In Use MOVING vs STORED vs BEING PROCESSED
Data Anonymization vs Data Masking IRREVERSIBLE vs format-preserving with FAKE data

NIST AI RMF functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage — Not OWASP, not MITRE. Don't mix up your frameworks.

Night Before Checklist

  • Review the 4 domain cheat sheets 30 min
  • Quick pass through the framework comparison matrix 10 min
  • Run through acronym speed drill 5 min
  • Skim the attack taxonomy 10 min
  • Get sleep — don't cram

If you don't know it by now, 2 more hours won't help. Sleep is more valuable than last-minute cramming. Your brain consolidates memories during sleep. Give it time to work.

Test Day Checklist

  • Bring two forms of ID — primary (government-issued photo ID) and secondary
  • Arrive 15 minutes early — you'll need time to check in and settle
  • Brain dump immediately — as soon as you sit down, write memorized frameworks, acronyms, and key facts on scratch paper before starting
  • Read every question carefully — CompTIA loves subtle wording differences
  • Don't change answers unless certain — your first instinct is usually right
  • Use all your time — don't rush to leave. Review is always worth it.

Brain dump priority order: NIST AI RMF functions, EU AI Act risk levels, attack taxonomy categories, key acronyms. Get these on paper while they're fresh.
