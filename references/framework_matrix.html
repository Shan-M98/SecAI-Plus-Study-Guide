When to Use Which Framework

Building an LLM chatbot?
Start with OWASP LLM Top 10 to address prompt injection, data leakage, and other LLM-specific risks.
Securing an ML pipeline?
Use OWASP ML Security Top 10 for data poisoning, model theft, and ML-specific vulnerabilities.
Red teaming your AI system?
Use MITRE ATLAS — the ATT&CK-style matrix maps adversarial tactics and techniques specifically for AI.
Conducting a broad AI risk assessment?
Reference the MIT AI Risk Repository for a comprehensive catalog of technical, ethical, and societal risks.
Deploying AI in the European Union?
EU AI Act compliance is mandatory. Classify your system's risk tier and meet the corresponding requirements.
Need enterprise risk management for AI?
Follow the NIST AI RMF and its four functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage.
Aligning with international AI policy?
Adopt OECD AI Principles — endorsed by 40+ countries for transparency, accountability, and human oversight.
Seeking formal AI certification?
Use ISO AI Standards (42001, 23894) — the only option that provides formal certification pathways.

Exam Quick-Reference Tips

  • OWASP = vulnerability lists (LLM Top 10 for LLMs, ML Top 10 for ML systems)
  • MITRE ATLAS = adversarial threat matrix (think ATT&CK but for AI)
  • MIT = risk catalog (broadest scope, academic research)
  • EU AI Act = the only legally binding one — 4 tiers: unacceptable, high, limited, minimal
  • NIST AI RMF = 4 functions — Govern, Map, Measure, Manage
  • OECD = international principles (voluntary, 40+ countries)
  • ISO = certification (42001 for AI management, 23894 for AI risk management)
  • If a question asks about legal requirements, the answer is EU AI Act
  • If a question asks about red teaming or threat modeling, the answer is MITRE ATLAS
  • If a question asks about risk management lifecycle, the answer is NIST AI RMF
